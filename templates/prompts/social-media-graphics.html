Aspect Ratios and Dimensions Across Platforms

Each social platform has its own optimal dimensions that affect how your content displays and performs in user feeds. Understanding these specifications ensures your generated content looks professional and captures maximum attention.

  • Instagram Feed Posts: 1080 x 1350 pixels (4:5 aspect ratio) is the ideal vertical format. This dimension allows for full-screen viewing on mobile devices and performs best in the feed algorithm. For square posts, use 1080 x 1080 pixels (1:1 ratio), which works well for brand consistency.
  • Instagram Stories: 1080 x 1920 pixels (9:16 aspect ratio). Stories occupy the full mobile screen, making this format essential for immersive, eye-catching content.
  • Instagram Reels: 1080 x 1920 pixels (9:16 aspect ratio). Reels compete for attention in a crowded feed, so full-screen vertical video dominates performance metrics.
  • TikTok Videos: 1080 x 1920 pixels (9:16 aspect ratio). TikTok is exclusively mobile-first, making vertical video the only viable format. The platform's algorithm heavily favors native vertical content.
  • LinkedIn Posts: 1200 x 627 pixels (16:9 aspect ratio) for carousels and image posts. LinkedIn's feed displays more horizontally oriented content, though 1080 x 1350 vertical posts also perform well for carousel ads.
  • LinkedIn Stories: 1080 x 1920 pixels (9:16 aspect ratio), though Stories are less prominent on LinkedIn compared to Instagram and TikTok.

Lighting and Visual Style Specifications

The aesthetic quality of your social media content heavily influences engagement rates. Different platforms and audiences respond to distinct lighting setups and visual styles. When using the AI generator, specifying these parameters ensures consistency with your brand voice and platform conventions.

  • Bright, Natural Lighting: Ideal for lifestyle, wellness, and educational content on Instagram and LinkedIn. Overcast daylight or soft window light creates flattering, approachable visuals without harsh shadows.
  • High Contrast and Saturation: TikTok audiences respond to bold, vibrant colors and high-energy visuals. Increased saturation and contrast help content stand out in the fast-scrolling feed.
  • Warm Color Grading: Instagram typically favors warm, golden-hour color temperatures that evoke aspiration and lifestyle. This applies particularly to fashion, beauty, and travel niches.
  • Cool, Professional Tones: LinkedIn content performs best with clean, professional color grading. Cool blues and grays convey trustworthiness and corporate credibility.
  • Minimalist vs. Dynamic Backgrounds: LinkedIn content benefits from uncluttered, professional backgrounds. Instagram and TikTok allow for more dynamic, visually busy backgrounds that capture attention.
  • Motion and Animation: TikTok and Instagram Reels heavily favor animated, fast-paced content. Specify parameters like "dynamic transitions," "fast cuts," or "kinetic typography" for maximum engagement.

Platform-Specific Style Recommendations

Each platform attracts audiences with different aesthetic preferences and content consumption patterns. Tailoring your prompt parameters to platform culture significantly improves performance metrics and follower engagement.

  • Instagram Aesthetic: Aspiring, lifestyle-focused, carefully curated. Emphasize high production value, flattering photography angles, and cohesive color palettes. Use parameters like "editorial quality," "fashion-forward," "Instagram-worthy," or "aesthetic composition."
  • TikTok Aesthetic: Authentic, spontaneous, trend-driven, playful. Include parameters like "casual," "behind-the-scenes," "trending sound visualization," "quick-cut editing," or "Gen-Z appeal." Humor and relatability outweigh polish.
  • LinkedIn Aesthetic: Professional, data-driven, authoritative, industry-relevant. Specify "corporate design," "professional infographic," "thought leadership," "business-focused," or "industry-standard typography." Credibility and expertise matter most.

The Best Negative Prompts

Negative prompts are crucial for filtering out undesired elements that can diminish engagement or brand credibility. These exclusions prevent the AI from generating content that conflicts with platform norms, audience expectations, or brand values. Always include relevant negative parameters to refine your output quality.

Universal Negative Parameters

  • Avoid Low-Quality Aesthetics: "No pixelated images, blurry text, poor lighting, amateur quality, low resolution, degraded colors, washed-out appearance, unprofessional editing."
  • Prevent Off-Brand Elements: "No inconsistent brand colors, mismatched fonts, conflicting design styles, outdated aesthetics, generic stock photo appearance, overused templates."
  • Exclude Engagement Killers: "No clickbait, misleading headlines, sensationalized claims, excessive emoji spam, overuse of hashtags, desperate tone, overly salesy language."
  • Remove Accessibility Issues: "No tiny unreadable text, insufficient color contrast, flashing lights, inaccessible design, no alt-text compatibility, poor text-to-image ratio."

Platform-Specific Negative Parameters

  • Instagram Exclusions: "No corporate jargon, overly filtered appearance, fake engagement pods evidence, spammy comments, bot-like content, outdated filter effects, low-effort memes, excessive watermarks."
  • TikTok Exclusions: "No overly polished corporate tone, outdated trends, slow pacing, generic motivation quotes, obvious advertising, forced dancing, irrelevant lip-sync, poor audio quality, vertical black bars."
  • LinkedIn Exclusions: "No casual slang, lowercase aesthetic, meme culture, entertainment-focused tone, oversimplification of complex topics, trendchasing, emotional manipulation, lack of data/sources, humble-bragging, emojis (except minimal use), hashtag stuffing."

Content-Specific Exclusions

  • E-commerce/Product Posts: "No unrealistic promises, fake testimonials, misleading product angles, artificial enhancement, competitor references, price visibility without context, out-of-stock indicators, cheap appearance."
  • Educational Content: "No oversimplification of complex concepts, factual inaccuracies, outdated information, condescending tone, gatekeeping language, lack of credibility markers, no sources cited, unclear takeaways."
  • Lifestyle Content: "No inauthentic life representation, extreme filters, unattainable lifestyles, tone-deaf aspirationalism, cultural appropriation, environmental insensitivity, exploitative imagery, excessive perfectionism."
  • B2B/Professional Content: "No vague jargon, lack of specificity, missing data/evidence, unprofessional language, contradictory messaging, poor citation, unclear value proposition, generic corporate speak."

FAQ

What aspect ratio should I use for maximum reach on TikTok versus Instagram Reels?
Both TikTok and Instagram Reels favor the 9:16 (1080 x 1920 pixels) vertical aspect ratio, as they're designed for mobile-first consumption. However, TikTok's algorithm treats native vertical video slightly differently than imported content—content filmed directly on TikTok typically receives better algorithmic distribution. For maximum reach on TikTok, film vertically or upload content without black bars. Instagram Reels also perform well with this dimension, but the platform shows slightly more flexibility. If you're repurposing content across both platforms, 9:16 is the universal safe choice. For LinkedIn, switch to 16:9 (1200 x 627 pixels) as LinkedIn's feed displays more horizontally, though vertical content does perform acceptably in carousel formats.
How do I balance professional credibility with authentic, relatable content on LinkedIn?
LinkedIn audiences increasingly value authentic vulnerability over sterile corporate messaging. The key is balancing expertise with humanity. In your prompts, specify "professional yet personable," "thought leadership with authentic voice," or "industry expertise with relatable insight." Include behind-the-scenes elements, challenge acknowledgments, and learning-from-failure narratives. Use parameters like "conversational expertise," "approachable authority," or "genuine professional perspective." This doesn't mean LinkedIn content should mimic TikTok's casual tone—maintain professional language, proper grammar, and industry credibility—but show your human side. Mention specific experiences, use inclusive language, and acknowledge different viewpoints. This combination generates significantly higher engagement than purely corporate content while maintaining your professional reputation.
Which negative prompts should I prioritize to improve my engagement rate?
The most impactful negative prompts depend on your platform and goals, but three universally boost performance: First, exclude "clickbait, misleading headlines, or sensationalized claims"—audiences quickly unfollow accounts that over-promise. Second, remove "generic stock photo appearance, low-effort memes, or outdated trends"—originality and freshness drive algorithmic favor. Third, eliminate "poor accessibility including tiny text, low color contrast, or inaccessible design"—better accessibility naturally increases audience reach and dwell time. For Instagram, also exclude "bot-like engagement patterns and spammy indicators." For TikTok, eliminate "corporate jargon and overly polished appearance." For LinkedIn, remove "excessive emoji use, casual slang, and unsubstantiated claims." Test different negative prompts with A/B testing to identify which exclusions most improve your specific audience's engagement rates.
