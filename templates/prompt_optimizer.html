Master the Art of Prompting

🎬 Lighting & Atmosphere

Professional images rely heavily on lighting to create mood and depth. When describing lighting in your prompts, be specific about the source and quality.

  • Specify light sources: "golden hour sunlight," "studio lighting," "moonlight," "neon glow"
  • Describe the mood: "warm and intimate," "dramatic and moody," "bright and cheerful"
  • Add atmospheric effects: "volumetric lighting," "lens flare," "atmospheric haze," "god rays"
  • Consider time of day: "dawn light," "twilight," "midday harsh shadows," "soft overcast"

📐 Composition & Angles

The angle and framing of your image dramatically impact its visual impact and storytelling power. Master these photographic principles to guide your AI generations.

  • Use camera terminology: "wide-angle shot," "close-up portrait," "overhead view," "Dutch angle"
  • Reference artistic movements: "Renaissance composition," "Art Deco style," "minimalist layout"
  • Specify depth: "shallow depth of field," "tack-sharp focus," "bokeh background," "layered depth"
  • Frame the subject: "rule of thirds," "centered composition," "leading lines," "negative space"

🚫 The Power of Negative Prompts

Sometimes what you don't want is just as important as what you do. Negative prompts guide the AI away from common mistakes and unwanted elements.

  • Exclude quality issues: "avoid blurry," "no distorted proportions," "no weird fingers," "no duplicate objects"
  • Remove unwanted styles: "not anime," "not cartoon," "not illustrated," "not low resolution"
  • Specify artistic boundaries: "no oversaturation," "avoid plastic look," "no artificial appearance"
  • Control composition: "not cluttered," "no busy background," "avoid watermarks," "no text artifacts"
